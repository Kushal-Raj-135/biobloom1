Crop Rotation & Air Quality: A Sustainable Solution

üå±

The Impact

Traditional farming practices like crop residue burning contribute to 30-40% of air pollution in agricultural regions. Our smart crop rotation system helps farmers break this cycle while improving their yields.

üí°

Our Solution

By implementing scientific crop rotation, we help reduce the need for residue burning by up to 80%. Different crops use different nutrients and leave behind beneficial residues that naturally enrich the soil.

üìà

Key Benefits

  • Reduces air pollution from crop burning
  • Improves soil health naturally
  • Increases crop yield by 20-30%
  • Reduces chemical fertilizer usage

The Science

Our AI-powered system analyzes soil type, climate, and previous crops to recommend optimal rotation patterns. This scientific approach ensures both environmental and economic benefits for farmers.

Understanding the Data

This graph shows monthly AQI levels comparing traditional farming vs. crop rotation practices. Lower AQI values indicate better air quality, demonstrating how crop rotation reduces air pollution through sustainable practices.

Peak Seasons

Notice the higher AQI levels during October-November (harvest season) without crop rotation due to stubble burning. Crop rotation methods show significantly lower peaks during these critical periods.

Environmental Impact

Areas practicing crop rotation show up to 40% reduction in average AQI levels, contributing to cleaner air and healthier communities. This improvement is most notable during traditional burning seasons.

Health Benefits

The reduced AQI levels through crop rotation mean fewer respiratory issues, better visibility, and improved overall public health in farming communities.
